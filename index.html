Introduction

This year, our theme is Metamorphosis. A quiet, beautiful transformation. The life cycle of a butterfly. It begins as something so small — an egg. Then it grows into a caterpillar, constantly feeding, changing, not quite knowing what it will become. After that comes the chrysalis stage, where everything slows down. Hidden. Still. But inside, everything is shifting. And finally… the butterfly emerges. Complete. Ready.

This idea shaped everything you are about to see.

Each dish in this experience reflects a stage of transformation. A moment in that journey. If you tap forward, you’ll discover how every plate connects to this theme — through ingredients, technique, and story.

But this is not just about butterflies.

Today is also a moment of change for all of us. As culinary students, this is our last day here. The end of something familiar. And maybe the beginning of something a little unknown too.

In a way, we are all in our own metamorphosis.

We came in learning, experimenting, making mistakes. Slowly growing. And now, we are stepping out, a little more ready than before. Ready to take that first flight, even if it feels uncertain.

This is our tribute. To the journey. To the growth. And to everyone here who has been part of it.

Thank you for being here with us.

Next: the egg

Stage one — the egg

Beet Cured Deviled Egg

Our first dish begins at the very start — the egg stage.

The egg white is cured in beetroot and red wine, giving it a subtle pickled flavour and a soft pink hue. Inside, the yolk is rich and creamy, blended with cheese for depth and smoothness. It’s finished with dill salt and toasted pistachio, adding freshness and a gentle crunch.

Visually, it’s delicate. Light. Almost quiet.

This dish represents the very beginning of transformation — just like the first day of a student. New, unaware, still forming. Not knowing what’s ahead, but already in the process of becoming.

Next: the caterpillar

Stage two — the caterpillar

Carrot Mosaic Terrine

The next stage — the caterpillar.

This terrine is shaped to resemble a caterpillar, symbolizing growth and constant movement. The carrot mosaic terrine is structured yet delicate, paired with a cauliflower porcini sauce that adds depth and earthiness. Sauce brings freshness and a slight bite, while the candied hazelnut salad adds sweetness and crunch. Yellow mustard pickles cut through with a sharp acidity.

It’s more developed. More active.

Like a culinary student in the middle of the journey — learning fast, experimenting, making mistakes, but growing every single day.

Next: the chrysalis

Stage three — the chrysalis

Smoked Salmon Mousse

The next stage — the chrysalis.

This dish is rich and layered. A smoked salmon mousse forms the base, smooth and deeply flavoured, topped with an even creamier, cheesy mousse. A hint of lemon zest cuts through the richness, while pickled red onions on top add sharpness and balance.

It’s a stage of quiet transformation.

Like college life when things start getting serious. Less chaos, more focus. You’re still figuring things out, but something is changing inside. Slowly becoming more refined, more sure — even if it’s not fully visible yet.

Next: the butterfly

Stage four — the butterfly

Butterfly Shrimp Bisque

The final stage — the butterfly.

A grilled butterfly shrimp, marinated and full of flavour, served with a rich, velvety shrimp bisque. Alongside, a light frisée salad with honey thyme vinaigrette adds freshness and balance to the dish.

It’s complete. Open. Confident.

Like us, at the end of this journey. No longer just learning, but ready. Ready to step out, take risks, and finally take that first flight.

Next: the first landing

The moment after — the first landing

Beetroot Carpaccio

A delicate beetroot carpaccio, finished with a balsamic glaze, crushed hazelnuts, and a smooth goat cheese sphere. Topped with a light shaved fennel salad, it’s fresh, balanced, and slightly earthy. This dish represents the flower — where the butterfly takes its first pause after flight.

Like us, stepping into the real world. Not just flying, but finding where we belong. Settling, exploring, and starting to build something of our own.

Next: complexity

The final expression — complexity

Pork Confit

A slow-poached pork confit, tender and rich, served with glazed baby carrots, smooth parsnip purée, and a sharp raspberry gastrique to balance it all.

This dish reflects the deeper stage of transformation.

Like adulthood. More layered, more demanding, but also more refined. Like a butterfly that has flown, struggled, and now understands how to navigate the world — with strength, balance, and purpose.
